There are very few places where you can watch a sperm whale surface sixty metres away and then look up to see snow-capped mountains. Kaikōura is one of them.

The town sits wedged between the Seaward Kaikōura Range and the Pacific. The mountains drop almost vertically to the highway. On the other side, the ocean opens wide and cold and blue. The drive up from Christchurch — two and a half hours north — is itself worth making.

The whales are here because a submarine canyon brings cold, deep water rich in squid to the surface year-round. This makes it one of the few places on Earth where you can reliably see sperm whales from shore. I booked the early morning tour and stood at the bow in the pale gold of 7am, slightly underdressed, entirely awake.

Activity Details

Location: Kaikoura Airport
Cost: 250 NZD
Duration: 3 hours

The Kaikōura Peninsula Walkway takes three hours and goes around the edge of the peninsula. Fur seals sleep on the rocks in improbable quantities. The views north toward the mountains are the best in New Zealand, which is saying something.

Food

The Kaikōura Seafood BBQ at the pier does crayfish tails cooked to order, with chips and aioli on the side. It is not cheap. It is the best thing I ate all year. Order two.

Distance from Christchurch: 180 km, approximately 2.5 hours north · Best for: Wildlife, coastal walking, seafood
