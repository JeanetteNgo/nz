Featured on National Geographic's list of most epic day hikes, the Tongariro Alpine Crossing is undoubtedly a must-do in New Zealand. Get ready to traverse through otherworldly volcanic landscapes, which served as the backdrop for Mordor in the Lord of the Rings films.

Doing the trek requires a booking with DOC, a relatively recent introduction in 2023 to better monitor visitor numbers. At the time of my booking in January 2025, there were no visitor caps and booking was free. However, I believe this may be changing in the near future, so please do check the requirements on the official DOC site before your visit.

Lodging and Transport

There are many viable lodging options in the nearby towns of Whakapapa, Turangi and National Park, with shuttle services available from each. I stayed at an airbnb at National Park, and had no complaints.

For transport, I booked a shuttle with Tongariro Crossing Shuttles, which costed $61.14 NZD. I chose a pickup time of 7:15 AM at a hostel not far from where I was staying.

The Hike

Distance: 21.3km one-way
Duration: ~6-8 hours
Difficulty: Hard

It is not an easy hike and there are multiple signboards in place to remind you of that, so be warned. The hike starts with a steady climb through alpine terrain, with views of the surrounding volcanic peaks. The first major landmark is the Red Crater, where you can see the steaming vents and vibrant red rocks. From there, you descend into the South Crater before ascending again to reach the highlight of the trek: the Emerald Lakes.

The Looong Descent

The descent to the Emerald Lakes involves loose scree that requires your full attention and probably your hands at least once. But then you arrive. Three pools of impossible blue-green water sitting in a cinder field as if dropped from somewhere else entirely. I sat above them for twenty minutes eating my sandwiches and felt not one shred of guilt about it.

The final descent into Ketetahi is through bush, which after four hours of exposed volcanic terrain feels almost absurdly pleasant. Bellbirds calling. Actual shade.
