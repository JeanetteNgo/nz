About Me

A working holiday, a year of wandering, a blog about both

Jeanette

Hi from Singapore!

I came to New Zealand on a working holiday visa in January 2024, with a vague plan to stay for a few months. Twelve months later I was still here, having worked a handful of jobs, driven most of the country's main roads, hiked fourteen trails, and eaten a truly unreasonable number of meat pies.

This blog is my attempt to document it honestly — not the Instagram version of travel, but the actual one. The getting-lost bits, the hard bits, the bits where the mountain is so beautiful you can't talk for a moment.

Year at a Glance
Mar 2024
Arrived in Christchurch
First stop in NZ. Settled in, explored, got a feel of the city. Dealt with admin, planned our next route, bought a car.
Apr 2024
Haast
A housekeeping stint that evolved into a web design assignment. Got my first taste of hiking in NZ.
May 2024
Nelson
Stayed with a local mountain biking coach and his family. Did video editing. Scrumptious meals everyday.
Kaikoura
My favourite town hands down! Did whale watching, seal kayaking, hiked my first mountain.
Jun - Sep 2024
Hanmer Springs
Undertook another web/brand design project. Enjoyed the Hot Springs so much we became regulars :) Did a bungy jump.
Sep 2024
Methven
Signed up for ski lessons. Woke up to views of Mt Hutt everyday.
Oct 2024
Kaiapoi
Worked on a local family farm. Weeded strawberry beds, planted flower seeds, tried chopping firewood. Gazed at grazing lambs every day. Dyed my hair blue.
Oct - Nov 2024
Trip with Friends
Went on a 2-week trip with friends. Checked off all the tourist spots: Lake Tekapo, Mount Cook, Milford Sound, Queenstown, Wanaka.

Made our way to the North Island. Got into my first car accident. Sorted things out and continued our journey through Wellington and on to Auckland.
Nov - Dec 2024
Whangārei
Stayed with a lovely landlady while the car was getting fixed. Took the chance to explore Northland. Made the trip to Cape Reinga. Went to the local farmer's market. Had the best strawberries of my life.

Tried snorkeling, parasailing, canyoning, sandboarding, surfing.
Jan 2025
Coromandel
Start of our "tourist adventure" arc. Did the Pinnacles. Slurped fresh oysters. Went to Cathedral Cove.
Tauranga
Explored Mt Maunganui. Tried a paddleboard. Went to Hobbiton.
Rotorua
Checked out the Red Woods. Rafted down a waterfall. Learned more about maori culture. Visited many geothermal sites. Ate the best hāngī of my life.
Taupo
Fun fact: Lake Taupo's about the size of Singapore :) Went on a sailboat tour. Visited the World's Coolest McDonald's.
National Park
Did many hikes, including one of the best day hikes in the world aka the Tongariro Alpine Crossing.
Feb 2025
Oamaru
Camped till late and spotted the little blue penguins. Am satisfied.
Dunedin
Visited Moeraki Boulders Beach. Walked up the steepest street in the world.
The Catlins
High key underrated place. Visited the Nugget Point lighthouse and Cathedral Caves. Also checked out the many waterfalls in the area.
Bluff
Visited Slope Point, the southernmost accessible point of South Island.
Te Anau
Solo drive to hike Key Summit. Toured the Te Anau Glow worm caves.
Wanaka
Tried mountain carting at Cadrona. Sat by the lake and read a book. I think about this moment a lot.
Fox Glacier
Did a glacier heli-hike.
Castle Hill
Stayed at a cosy ski lodge. Watched the sun rise. Wandered among limestone boulders that served as the backdrop for a battle scene in Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe.
Feb - Mar 2025
Trip with Family
Went on a 2-week trip with family to round off our working holiday.

Revisited favourite spots in the South Island: Hanmer Springs, Kaikoura, Lake Tekapo, Mount Cook, Wanaka, Queenstown.

Went on many hikes including the Kaikoura Peninsula Walkway, Hooker Valley, Sealy Tarns, Roys Peak. Skydived as a family :) Did the Nevis Swing. Tried hanggliding.
11 Mar 2025
Back to Singapore
I've been dreading the return date, although I knew it would inevitably come. Even now, it's not a feeling I can express properly in words.

It took me a long, long time to shake off the foreign feeling when I landed in Singapore and walked down the same neighbourhoods I always did before I left. Things looked the same, and yet, nothing was the same.

One year on, I still look back at my time in NZ with longing and a twinge of surreality. Small comfort, but I promised myself that I'll be back one day, and I intend to keep that promise.
