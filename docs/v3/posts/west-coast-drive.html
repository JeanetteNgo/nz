The West Coast of the South Island receives more rainfall than almost anywhere else in New Zealand. It is green in the way that only places that get a lot of rain can be green — almost aggressive in its greenness, dripping and lush and improbable. I drove it in September and it rained every day.

I would do it again immediately.

Hokitika

Start here. The Sunset Point beach walk takes twenty minutes and gives you a view of the Tasman Sea with the Southern Alps immediately behind the town, snowcapped. This combination — beach and glacier-capped mountains — should not be geographically possible, and yet here it is.

Hokitika Gorge, half an hour inland, has water of the most extraordinary blue-green I've seen in any river. The colour comes from glacial flour — finely ground rock suspended in the water. Free to visit, short walk from the car park.

The glaciers

Franz Josef and Fox glaciers descend from the Southern Alps to within a few hundred metres of sea-level rainforest. They've been retreating significantly in recent decades, but the helicopter hike experience is still available and still extraordinary. I went by helicopter to the upper ice field above Franz Josef on a morning that cleared after rain. The ice was blue-white and silent. It felt important to see it now.

Franz Josef from the air: green rainforest, grey river, white ice, and above it all, the stripped rock of the upper Alps. All the colours of the South Island in one view.

Haast and the pass

The Haast Pass road, heading east toward Wānaka, is one of the most dramatic drives in New Zealand. The highway follows the Haast River through gorges of dripping jungle before climbing through the main divide. The Gates of Haast — a narrow canyon where the river forces itself through rock — warrants the stop.

  • The coast gets very wet — pack for rain regardless of forecast
  • Petrol stations are sparse; fill up in Hokitika or Franz Josef
  • The pancake rocks at Punakaiki (between Greymouth and Westport) are worth a stop
  • Book glacier flights ahead — clear weather is unpredictable and slots fill

Route: Greymouth → Hokitika → Franz Josef → Haast → Wānaka · Driving time: 4–5 hours without stops
