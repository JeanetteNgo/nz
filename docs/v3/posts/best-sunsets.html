A completely subjective ranking of the best places I watched the sun go down during twelve months in Aotearoa. In approximate order of how urgently I wanted to phone someone who wasn't there.

1. Lake Tekapo, Mackenzie Basin

The Church of the Good Shepherd silhouetted against a sky that starts pale yellow and ends purple through every shade of pink and orange. The lake is turquoise. The Mackenzie Basin is so far from light pollution that stars become visible while there's still colour in the west. I sat on the grass above the church for two hours and missed dinner and did not mind at all.

Worth noting: Tekapo sunsets are not reliable. The basin gets high cloud. When it clears though, there's nothing like it.

2. Mount Roy summit, Wānaka

A four-hour return walk above the town brings you to a ridge where Lake Wānaka and Lake Hāwea are both visible simultaneously. The late afternoon light hits the water and makes the colour shift from blue to gold. The walk down in fading light requires a headtorch, which I had. Bring one.

3. Tongariro plateau

The red cinder, the sulphur yellow, the steam from vents — the alien quality of it is amplified by low sunlight. Everything goes theatrical. I saw this on the crossing, and stayed on the ridge twenty minutes longer than I planned to.

4. Waiheke Island looking back at Auckland

From an eastern vineyard, glass of Syrah in hand, watching the city lights come on across the Hauraki Gulf while the sky above goes its own colours over the water. This is slightly cheating because the wine helps. It is still beautiful.

5. Queenstown from Bob's Peak

Lake Wakatipu and the Remarkables. The mountains do pink-and-gold above the lake. The town below lights up. Queenstown knows exactly how good this looks and has not overdeveloped the view, which is to its credit.

New Zealand has too many good sunsets. This is not a problem most places have.

Honourable mentions: Cape Reinga in the far north where two seas meet; Franz Josef alpenglow on the peaks after sunset; Coromandel hot water beach at dusk.
